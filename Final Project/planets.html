Planets in Our Solar System

  • Mercury

    Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has extreme temperature variations, with a day lasting 176 Earth days. It has no atmosphere to retain heat.

    Distance from the Sun: 57.9 million km

  • Venus

    Venus is known for its thick atmosphere, which creates a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system. It rotates backwards compared to other planets.

    Distance from the Sun: 108.2 million km

  • Earth

    Our home planet, Earth, is the only known planet to support life. It has a perfect mix of atmosphere, water, and temperature to sustain life.

    Distance from the Sun: 149.6 million km

  • Mars

    Mars, known as the Red Planet, has been the subject of human interest for potential colonization. It has a thin atmosphere and seasonal dust storms.

    Distance from the Sun: 227.9 million km

  • Jupiter

    Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant with a strong magnetic field and famous for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has lasted for centuries.

    Distance from the Sun: 778.3 million km

  • Saturn

    Saturn is known for its iconic ring system, which is made of ice and rock particles. It’s the second-largest planet in our solar system.

    Distance from the Sun: 1.43 billion km

  • Uranus

    Uranus is a gas giant that rotates on its side, making its axis of rotation almost parallel to its orbit. It’s often called an “ice giant” due to its cold atmosphere.

    Distance from the Sun: 2.87 billion km

  • Neptune

    Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and has the strongest winds in the solar system. Its atmosphere is mostly composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

    Distance from the Sun: 4.5 billion km

Planets Beyond Our Solar System

Exoplanets, or planets outside our solar system, are being discovered regularly. Scientists have found thousands of them, and many are in the habitable zone of their stars.

  • Kepler-452b

    Kepler-452b, also known as Earth’s cousin, is an exoplanet that orbits in the habitable zone of its star. It's the most Earth-like planet discovered so far.

    Distance from Earth: 1,400 light-years

  • Proxima Centauri b

    Proxima Centauri b orbits the closest star to our Sun, Proxima Centauri. It’s located in the habitable zone, making it a potential candidate for life.

    Distance from Earth: 4.24 light-years

  • TRAPPIST-1

    TRAPPIST-1 is a star system with seven Earth-sized planets, three of which are in the habitable zone, potentially able to support life.

    Distance from Earth: 39.5 light-years
